Spark Knowledge Discovery Tool Prototype

The Spark Knowledge Discovery Tool (1) leverages findings from research to assist users in discovering new information. Serendipitous Knowledge Discovery (SKD), a widely-researched information-seeking behavior, is the coincidental discovery of useful information. The graphs produced by this tool consist of assertions, in the form of subject-predicate-object semantic predications. These predications, a product of SemRep (2), are extracted from PubMed citations.



Basic Instructions:

  • Enter a query in the text box near the upper-left corner, by Enter Concept(s), and click Search.
  • Click on one of the five randomization options to the right (the All, Common, or Rare, buttons, or from one of the drop-down menus). This will produce an SKD graph of manageable size, composed of semantic predications in links and nodes.
  • Click on a link between nodes in the graph to view the semantic predication, the original text, and PubMed access; click again to hide them.
  • Hover the cursor over a link to just see its full semantic predication.
  • Right-Click on the central node to select an alternative central topic from the resulting menu.
  • Double-Click on a link to transfer its semantic predication to the workspace on the right. Once transferred, you can still access the sentence by clicking on a link, or the full semantic predication by hovering the cursor over the link, in the same fashion.
  • Manipulate graph elements by dragging nodes.
  • Use the buttons on the bottom to discard, save, or reopen graphs. Click on a semantic predication's link in the workspace and click Delete Predication to delete a single predication.
