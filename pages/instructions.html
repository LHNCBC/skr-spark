SKD Discovery Tool

The Serendipitous Knowledge Discovery Tool leverages findings from research to assist users in discovering new information (1). Serendipitous Knowledge Discovery, a widely-researched information-seeking behavior, is the coincidental discovery of useful information.



Basic Instructions:

  • Enter a query in the text box on the top-left, by Enter Concept(s), and click Search
  • Click on one of the five randomization options to the right (the All, Common, or Rare, buttons, or from one of the drop-down menus). This will produce an SKD graph of manageable size, composed of semantic predications
  • Click on a link to view the sentence and PubMed access; click again to hide them
  • Hover the mouse over a link to just see its semantic predication
  • Control-Click on a link to transfer its predication to the workspace on the right
  • Manipulate these transferred predications as desired, by dragging the nodes
  • Use the buttons on the bottom to discard, save, or reopen graphs. Click on a predication link in the workspace and click Delete Predication to delete a single predication.


(1) See Workman, et al., Framing serendipitous information-seeking behavior for facilitating literature-based discovery: A proposed model JASIST March 2014 65(3): 501-512.
